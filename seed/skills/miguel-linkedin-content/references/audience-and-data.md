# Audience and Data

This file captures who Miguel's audience actually is and what his real post performance has been. Treat the data here as more authoritative than general LinkedIn advice. Patterns that worked on his account beat patterns that worked on someone else's account, every time.

## Who his audience actually is

Miguel's positioning is **"AI Research & Full-Stack Engineer | AI systems for real-world operations and teams."**

His target ICP is operators and founders at small-to-mid market companies — specifically:

- **Company size**: $1M–$50M annual revenue, 2–100 people
- **Roles**: Founder-CEOs, COOs, VPs of operations, ops directors, founder-brokers, multi-venture entrepreneurs
- **Industries that have responded best**: Real estate operations (property management, OaaS/flex workspace, multifamily, brokerage), industry associations, multi-site service businesses
- **Geography**: Primary US East Coast, secondary UK/Europe, tertiary UAE/Middle East. Miguel is Lisbon-based.

What this audience cares about: **shipping working systems**, not "AI transformation." They are skeptical of consultants, allergic to vague claims, and they value sharp specificity over thought leadership.

What this audience does NOT respond to:

- Generic "AI is changing everything" takes
- Posts that look like content marketing
- Engagement-farming CTA gates ("comment X to get the playbook")
- Posts that name them as the problem in a tone they would not show to a colleague

## Real performance data from his account

These are the actual numbers from posts Miguel has shipped. Use them to calibrate predictions for future posts.

### Top performer

**"Most 'senior engineers' are about to discover they're not senior anymore."**

- 15,416 impressions
- 42 reactions, 54 comments
- Engagement rate: ~0.62%
- **Why it worked**: identity hook + threat + curiosity + pre-empted objections + two-path close + question close ("Which one are you on?")
- 54 comments on 15k impressions is the standout number — real engagement, real argument, real conversation. This is the pattern to study.

### Mid performer

**"AI is going to replace engineers in 18 months" (Microsoft Diffusion Report)**

- 3,377 impressions
- 20 reactions, 4 comments
- Engagement rate: ~0.71%
- **Why it underperformed expectations**: data-driven post that informed but did not provoke. People read it and moved on. The 4 comments tell you the post was not "argument-shaped."

### Underperformer (predicted 30k+, got 772)

**"AI strategist" is the 2026 version of a crypto bro with a better PowerPoint.**

- 772 impressions
- 4 reactions, 0 comments
- Engagement rate: ~0.52%
- **Why it failed**: posted too close to the senior engineer hit. Algorithm read same-shape-same-topic content from same creator and downranked. Also possible: "crypto bro" reference made it feel tribal rather than universal.
- **Lesson**: even great posts fail if cadence is wrong. Same-villain back-to-back posts get punished.

### Niche performer

**"The 'I built it in a weekend with AI' era is ending"**

- 459 impressions
- 6 reactions, 1 comment
- Engagement rate: ~1.52% (highest of the batch)
- **Why it matters**: small reach but the highest engagement RATE of any post. Tells you that his concentrated, serious audience responds to builder content — even when the algorithm doesn't push it widely. This is the audience that converts to DMs, not the audience that just reacts.

## What the data tells us

Three patterns emerged from the real numbers:

1. **Rage-bait + identity threat + concrete contrast hits hardest on first use.** Second use of the same shape against an adjacent target gets 5–10% of the original reach. This means: one rage-bait per 2 weeks max, never two on adjacent topics back-to-back.

2. **Data-driven posts inform but don't provoke.** They get views, not comments. If the goal is reach, fine. If the goal is engagement and DMs, the post needs to force a position, not just present data.

3. **Builder voice posts have lower reach but higher engagement rate.** They attract the right audience. For Miguel, the right audience is more valuable than the broad audience — he is selling to a specific ICP, not running ads.

## Posting time

**Tuesday or Wednesday, 1:00 PM Lisbon time** is the sweet spot. This translates to:

- 8:00 AM US Eastern (peak open-laptop window)
- 5:00 PM UAE (functional, end-of-workday scroll window)

Avoid Mondays (people catching up on overnight work), Fridays after noon Lisbon (US East still wakes up but the algorithm is winding down for weekend), and weekends (LinkedIn B2B traffic is ~30% of weekday).

## Profile baseline

Miguel's current profile baseline that every post lands against:

- Headline: "AI Research & Full-Stack Engineer | AI systems for real-world operations and teams"
- About: leads with "I build AI agents and automations that survive production"
- Featured: needs to be aligned with builder positioning — anyone clicking from a post evaluates this in 5 seconds

If the profile is not in line with what a post promises, the post leaks readers without converting them.
